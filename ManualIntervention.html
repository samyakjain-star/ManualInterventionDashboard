Three types of ops correction: Add only means routing didn't assign enough people to a trip — ops had to add more. Delete only means routing put too many people on a trip — ops removed the excess. Replace means routing got the number of people right but picked the wrong ones — ops swapped them out. Replace is the hardest problem to solve because it means the routing algorithm's logic for grouping employees together is off, not just its count.

Data quality
Employees dropping off trips automatically — not because of ops decisions, but because of upstream data changes

What is data intervention? When routing approves a trip, it locks in a set of employees. But sometimes, between routing and the trip actually running, something changes — an employee cancels their own roster booking, or their profile changes (they move teams, their transport access gets removed, their pickup stop changes). When this happens, the system automatically removes them from the trip. This is called a data-driven removal. It's not routing getting it wrong — it's the input data becoming stale after routing ran.

Double hit

Roster cancellations vs profile changes: Roster cancellations (331 cases) happen when an employee had booked a seat on a trip but cancelled it after routing ran. This is the employee changing their mind or plans. Profile changes (39 cases) happen when something in the employee's master data changed — they moved to a different team, their transport access was revoked, or their pickup location changed. Both make the routing output stale, but they need different fixes. Roster cancellations need tighter booking deadlines. Profile changes need faster data sync before routing runs.

Data intervention rate by client

69c649 has the highest data intervention at 23.8% — nearly 1 in 4 trips had an automatic employee drop. 694b93 is next at 17.4%. These clients have roster instability problems that need to be addressed at the HR/attendance system level, not the routing level. Clients like 683ed8 and 697af8 below 2% are well-managed — employees commit to their bookings and data stays stable.

Roster vs profile removals by client

Almost all data drops are roster cancellations (blue). Profile changes (teal) are rare and scattered. This means the primary upstream fix is roster management — reducing last-minute cancellations — rather than employee data hygiene.

Data intervention by day of week

Wednesday has the most data drops (6.8%), followed by Tuesday (6.0%). Weekend data drops are very low (1.0–1.5%). This suggests mid-week roster cancellations — employees booking trips at the start of the week and cancelling by mid-week when plans change — are the primary driver.

Unfixed data drops — trips that ran short

Unfixed drops are the most operationally significant — these are trips where an employee was automatically removed and ops never added anyone back. The trip ran with fewer people than planned. 61 trips (17.1% of all data-intervened trips) fell into this category. This is a capacity and service quality issue.

Weekly data intervention trend by client

694b93 shows persistently high data intervention every week (15–20%), suggesting a chronic roster management problem. 69c649 only appears in one week (23.8%) — either a one-off event or a new client with teething problems. Clients where data intervention is consistently low have good roster discipline — employees commit to bookings and don't cancel last-minute.
